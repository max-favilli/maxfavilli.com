## Post body

If you are a Claude Code user, /model claude-opus-4-6 may be your best friend for a while.

Opus 4.7 launched two days ago. 3,100 upvotes on "serious regression." The press coined "the Claude-lash." Meanwhile the benchmarks say coding improved by seven points.

Both things are true. But everyone is missing the real story.

[BOLD]The sycophancy problem.[/BOLD] People switched from ChatGPT to Claude because Opus pushed back. Challenged bad ideas. Flagged wrong assumptions early, when they are cheap to fix. Now 4.7 agrees with everything. A model that tells you what you want to hear is not a collaborator. It is a liability. Especially if the missed pushback is architectural — a data model, a service boundary — and you find out weeks later.

[BOLD]The compute crunch.[/BOLD] OpenAI told investors that Anthropic made a "strategic misstep to not acquire enough compute" and that it is "showing up in the product." The new tokenizer eats 35% more tokens. Adaptive thinking silently skips reasoning. 4.7 might not be a worse model — it might be a cheaper-to-run model dressed as an upgrade.

[BOLD]The price of coding agents will go dramatically up.[/BOLD] If a coding agent lets a developer finish in three days what used to take two months, a $200/month subscription is absurdly underpriced. Current pricing is the land grab — like ride-sharing subsidies before the prices went up. It will not last.
