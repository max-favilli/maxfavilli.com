## Post body

If companies only want senior engineers — because AI agents now do the work that used to train juniors — where does the next generation of seniors come from?

That's one of the questions in Part 2 of my "Future of AI" series. The others:

• Why the [BOLD]build-vs-buy line is shifting back toward build[/BOLD]. Shopify now requires teams to prove AI can't do the job before requesting headcount. Martin Alderson wrote: "Software ate the world. Agents are going to eat SaaS." Bain is asking whether agentic AI will disrupt the SaaS model entirely.

• Why [BOLD]Claude Sonnet 4.6 is a landmark moment[/BOLD] for AI coding — not "better autocomplete" but a different mode of working.

• Why [BOLD]prompts will replace code[/BOLD] for entire classes of problems. And why that flatly violates Simon Willison's rule: don't commit code you don't understand.

I wrote Part 1 in November 2025, before most of these pieces existed. Part 2 is in the first comment.
